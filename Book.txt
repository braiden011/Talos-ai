Human civilization began with the Neolithic Revolution when people started farming.
The first cities appeared in Mesopotamia between the Tigris and Euphrates rivers.
Ancient Egyptians built the Great Pyramids and developed a system of writing called hieroglyphics.
The Indus Valley civilization was famous for its advanced city planning and drainage systems.
In Ancient China, the Silk Road was created to trade goods like silk and spices with the West.
The Greeks invented democracy in Athens and made great progress in philosophy and science.
The Roman Empire created a massive system of roads and a legal code that influenced the modern world.
During the Middle Ages, the Feudal system was the main way society was organized in Europe.
The Renaissance was a time of great art and learning that started in Italy in the 1400s.
The Industrial Revolution changed the world by using steam engines and factories to make goods.
World War I and World War II were the largest conflicts in history and led to the creation of the United Nations.
The Space Age began in 1957 when the first satellite, Sputnik, was launched into orbit.
The Digital Revolution started in the late 2000s when the internet connected the entire world.
Modern human civilization now focuses on global trade, technology, and space exploration.
